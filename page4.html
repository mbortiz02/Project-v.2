Impact of COVID-19

COVID-19 dealt a huge amount of damage to the already problematic housing crisis in Brazil. The infection plagued low-income families living in major cities like SÃ£o Paulo and Rio de Janeiro. As the homelessness have little access to healthcare, shelter, and basic sanitory conditions, areas containing some of the poorest residents had some of the highest COVID-19 death rates at around 80 residents for every 100,000 residents.

Favelas, slums found in Brazil where high infection rates are found, has been calculated to contain "at least 5.1 million homes" in poor state, according to the Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE). As low-income families who live in Favelas already suffer from imbalance and all types of abuse, they cannot properly self-isolate themselves from the coronavirus and from others. This demonstrates the serevity of the housing crisis as a large number of people cannot afford to defend themselves from the coronavirus. Lockdown protocols shutting the city down make it even more difficult for the already struggling families to make a living.
To help combat the Pandemic amid the housing crisis, the Zero Evictions Campaign was launched in July 2020 to demand a temporary suspension to any activity that would obstruct the rights of any family and community from eviction.

New programs designed to aid the homeless by offering shelter were introduced while the government passes new laws on decriminalizing homelessness. Public Campaigns raised awareness and rallied people to advocate for change. City governments created multiple shelters for the homeless, including one specifically for those are ill with COVID-19.

From a different perspective, COVID-19 helped spread awareness of the housing crisis. The damage was so devastating that it only made clearer how bad the housing crisis was in Brazil and that change is desperately needed.
